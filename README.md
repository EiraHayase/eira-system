# Eira System

Eira System is a long-term AI research project dedicated to developing a next-generation AI-based risk prediction system.

The ultimate goal of Eira System is to develop an AI capable of identifying early warning signs before critical situations occur by integrating multiple types of real-world data.

This repository documents the early stages of the project's development.

Many critical events begin with subtle changes that people often overlook. Eira System explores whether AI can recognize those changes earlier by treating emotions, physical conditions, behavior, and everyday experiences as measurable data.

---

## Research Concept

One of the core ideas behind Eira System is to treat emotions as quantifiable variables rather than subjective experiences.

The project aims to transform emotional states into structured data and integrate them with physical condition, behavior, environmental factors, and life events.

Its objective is to investigate whether emotional variables can improve AI-based risk prediction by helping AI detect subtle warning signs before risks become critical.

Rather than treating emotions as abstract feelings, Eira System models them as quantifiable variables that can be analyzed alongside other forms of data.

---

## First Dataset

This repository contains the developer's first longitudinal dataset.

The purpose of this dataset is to establish the foundation for future AI models by recording daily changes in emotional, physical, behavioral, and environmental variables.

The dataset currently includes:

- Emotional variables
- Physical condition
- Sleep
- Menstrual cycle
- Daily activities
- Weather
- Stress events
- Personal notes

---

## Long-Term Vision

Eira System aims to establish a new approach to AI risk prediction by treating emotions as quantifiable variables alongside physical, behavioral, and environmental data.

By integrating these diverse variables, the project seeks to build AI capable of discovering hidden relationships, recognizing subtle warning signs, and predicting potential risks before they become critical.
